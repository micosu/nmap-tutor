Form for Online Student Assent

This is part of a research study conducted by Rotem Guttman at Carnegie Mellon University.

Summary

  • You are being asked to participate in a research study about learning with a web-based tutor.
  • Taking part in this study is voluntary. You were selected as a possible participant because you are taking Robb Duty’s Intro to Python course, and the tutor is embedded in the class.
  • We are a group of researchers from Carnegie Mellon University who are highly experienced with educational technology research, and running learning studies in schools.
  • We have obtained permission from City Charter High School to conduct a study, with your instructor’s permission.
  • In summary, you will engage with a web-based intelligent tutoring system (ITS) focused on teaching students core cybersecurity concepts and conducting network reconnaissance. This ITS is designed to complement what you are learning in class.
  • This form is asking whether or not you are okay with the data collected from this tutor, being used for research purposes

Purpose

The purpose of the study is to see whether or not the cybersecurity ITS is effective and to identify ways of improving the tool so it is more effective and equitable for all learners.

Procedures

The study will occur during the week of Nov. 17th, during normal class time. You will use the web-based tutor during the class period. You will also complete a short pre-test, and post-test during the class period to assess your learning during the activity.

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to students in your course (Intro to Python Programming). Aside from that, there are no other requirements to participate.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. For example, you might become bored while working through the tutor. We aim to prevent this by providing engaging, realistic scenarios – akin to situations you might encounter as a cybersecurity professional – along with immediate visual feedback. We will also contextualize the concepts covered in the tutor, in the larger context of your Python course. Your username won’t be linked to your name or other identifying information, so there is minimal risk of your data being de-identified or otherwise linked back to you. Only your instructor will have access to the mapping of student usernames to student identities. .

Benefits

You will gain the direct benefit of increased understanding of core cybersecurity concepts and basic technical skills in network reconnaissance. The knowledge received by this study will help improve EdTech development for high school students in STEM domains, ultimately supporting student learning.

Compensation & Costs

There is no compensation for participation in this study. There will be no cost to you, if you decide to participate in this study.

Future Use of Information

In the future, once we have removed all identifiable information from your data, we may use collected data for our future research studies, or we may distribute the data to other researchers for their research studies. We would do this without getting additional informed consent from you (or your legally authorized representative). Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be identified.

Confidentiality

By participating in this research, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon may be required to disclose your assent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner:

We will not collect student names or other demographic information. Only instructors will create and have access to a mapping of student names to anonymized usernames logging into the tool. Researchers will only receive the usernames. IP addresses and other location or device-specific information may be collected, but will be removed from processed student data.

Your data and assent form will be kept separate. Your assent form will be stored in a secure location on Carnegie Mellon property and will not be disclosed to third parties. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon to others outside of Carnegie Mellon. However, your or your child’s name, address, contact information and other direct personal identifiers will not be mentioned in any such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon. Note that per regulation all research data must be kept for a minimum of 3 years.
