Nmap Tutor - Research Consent

Before we begin, please take a moment to review the consent form below. Your response to the consent questions will be recorded.

Summary

In this research study,we are engaging some students enrolled in information technology courses in a series of exercises where they describe how they are completing class assignments, what problems they are encountering, and what tools or resources they find helpful. The student feedback will be used to design learning technologies that can accelerate and improve learning for future students. We hope that participation will offer the opportunity to interact with useful learning technologies, which may contribute to your academic progress in targeted courses. You may also receive an intangible benefit from contributing to research that advances learning science and educational technology design.

We are also measuring student learning in these courses to better understand the role of computer-based learning exercises. The learning gains of participating students will be recorded. These measures are collected solely for research purposes and will not have any impact on your grade.

Your participation in either aspect of this research study is purely voluntary. If you decline participation in the Think Aloud exercise, you will not be asked to complete it. If you decline participation in the part of the study focused on computer-based learning exercises, no data will be collected by this project regarding your interaction with that exercise.

Purpose

The purpose of the research is to better understand how learning technologies can be deployed to improve student learning in information technology courses.

Procedures

The think-aloud protocol will proceed in the following manner.  We will begin the first session by presenting a slideshow presentation to students, introducing them to the study. We will explain what a “think-aloud” exercise is, why we are utilizing this method, and provide a demonstration. Then, students will begin a course assignment while thinking aloud.  In the first session, students will complete the assignment without the existence of an intelligent tutor.   In the second round of “think-alouds,” students will complete the assignment with an intelligent tutor available. In either session, if the student stops speaking, we will prompt them to continue speaking: asking questions such as “Can you tell me what you’re looking for?” or “Can you say what you are thinking about?  After the participant is finished with the assignment, we will ask follow-up questions about the students’ general experience and about their interaction with the assignment.  The think-aloud sessions will be recorded on Zoom, and student interaction with an intelligent tutor will be logged.  However, only members of the study team will view audio and video recordings.  Any data shared with outside researchers will be limited to anonymized transcripts and/or anonymized log data capturing interaction with the intelligent tutor.  We expect that each round will last no more than 80 minutes, and that the number of participants in a session will not exceed 10. 

Some students will also engage directly in computer-based learning exercises that are designed to enhance comprehension and learning of key course concepts, without going through the think aloud exercise.  Other students will learn the material without using the computer-based learning exercise. 

We expect that completion of all online exercises undertaken without the think aloud process will require about 60 minutes in total. 

Participant Requirements

Participation in this study is limited to individuals age 18 and older who are enrolled in IT courses offered by community colleges using CMU technology, speak English, and are physically resident in the United States at the time they participate in this study.

Risks

The risks and discomfort associated with participation in this study are no greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during other online activities. You may encounter boredom or fatigue while undertaking this activity. There is a potential risk of breach of confidentiality. However, we will take every precaution to protect your confidentiality by maintaining study records with personally identifiable information in password protected files on the secure Carnegie Mellon University system, to which only study team members with appropriate training will have access. Any information shared with external researchers will be carefully deidentified. Anonymization efforts will extend to careful writing and multiple reviews of papers and reports before publication to ensure that no participant can be tied to a particular action, reaction, or statement.

Benefits

There are no direct benefits from your participation in this study. We hope that participation will offer the opportunity to interact with useful learning technologies, which may contribute to your academic progress in targeted courses. You may also receive an intangible benefit from contributing to research that brings benefit to the scientific community, by helping the researchers understand how educational technologies could better support and accelerate student learning in IT courses.

Compensation & Costs

There will be no cost to you if you participate in this study. Participants will be paid $45 via Paypal or Venmo for every think-aloud exercise completed. Students who use computer-based learning exercises will do so as part of their regular class and will not receive payment.

Future Use of Information

In the future, once we have removed all identifiable information from your data, we may use the data for our future research studies, or we may distribute the data to other researchers for their research studies. We would do this without getting additional informed consent from you (or your legally authorized representative). Sharing of data with other researchers will only be done in such a manner that you will not be identified.

Confidentiality

If you participate in this research, a Zoom recording will be made of your interaction with the Think Aloud exercise. If you engage directly with the computer-based learning exercises without a Think Aloud exercise, a Zoom recording may be made of that interaction. The Zoom platform is not owned by CMU. The company that owns this platform will have access to the recordings produced and any identifiable information you share with them while using their product. Please note that Carnegie Mellon does not control the Terms and Conditions of the company or how they will use any information that they collect.

However, the Zoom recordings and other information collected by Carnegie Mellon will be carefully protected. We will keep only one original and one backup copy of Zoom recordings in secure, password-protected file storage locations on the Carnegie Mellon computer system, which uses appropriate information security procedures to guard our data. Only members of the study team with human subjects training will have access to these recordings. The only data from these exercises that might be shared with outside researchers will be anonymized transcripts and/or anonymized log data from interaction with our intelligent tutor software that has been carefully edited to ensure the identity of the subject cannot be inferred. Anonymization efforts will extend to the careful writing and multiple reviews of papers and reports before publication to assure that no participant can be tied to a particular activity, comment, reaction, or statement.

By participating in this research, you understand and agree that Carnegie Mellon may be required to disclose your consent form, data and other personally identifiable information as required by law, regulation, subpoena or court order. Otherwise, your confidentiality will be maintained in the following manner:

Your data and consent form will be kept separate. Your consent form will be stored in a secure location on Carnegie Mellon property and will not be disclosed to third parties. By participating, you understand and agree that the data and information gathered during this study may be used by Carnegie Mellon and published and/or disclosed by Carnegie Mellon to others outside of Carnegie Mellon. However, your name, address, contact information and other direct personal identifiers will not be mentioned in any such publication or dissemination of the research data and/or results by Carnegie Mellon. Note that per regulation all research data must be kept for a minimum of 3 years. No annotation services will be used. The National Science Foundation, which sponsors this research, may have access to our research records.
